Antarctica does not contain any sovereign nations within it, because of the Antarctic Treaty System (ATS).

Some countries have Antarctic territory claims but they are not allowed to mine, or build any large infrastructure that would be disruptive to the landscape.

The only infrastructure in Antarctica is multiple scientific research bases, owned by many different countries across the globe.

The USA and Russia are the only two nations that are allowed to claim territory on Antacrtica if they decide to.

This means that the ATS renders Antarctica the world's largest nature reserve, as countries with territorial claims are not allowed to expand their claims in any way.

Marie Byrd Land is the only area of Antarctica unclaimed by any nation.
